Doctoral Researcher · Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering

Computational research for biofluids, turbomachinery and related engineering systems.

I am a PhD student in the Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at Gyeongsang National University (GNU), South Korea, and a doctoral researcher at the Flow Information Lab. My work combines Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), experimental validation, reduced-order modeling, and machine learning for practical engineering and biofluids applications.

Research Focus

Core areas and representative applications

Biofluids & Medical Twin

CFD and experimental analysis of physiological flows, including thrombin-induced clot formation in pseudoaneurysms, ureteral stent flow obstruction/encrustation, and upper-airway airflow for obstructive sleep apnea diagnosis.

Turbomachinery

Numerical and experimental studies of pump and propeller systems, including hydraulic performance optimization, cavitation/erosion analysis, booster-pump operation, and rim-driven propeller duct-shape effects.

Composite Materials

Natural fiber-reinforced composites, eco-friendly biopolymers, additive manufacturing, and materials for biomedical and structural engineering applications.

Machine Learning & Reduced-order Modeling

Integration of machine learning concepts such as Dynamic Mode Decomposition, Koopman theory, and reduced-order modeling to support faster diagnosis, simulation, and engineering decision-making.
