My research combines Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), experimental validation, reduced-order modeling, and machine learning for biofluids, turbomachinery, composite materials, and medical-twin applications.

Research

My research goal is to develop reliable simulation- and data-driven methods for understanding fluid-flow behavior in engineering and biomedical systems. I work mainly on CFD-based analysis, experimental validation, medical-twin modeling, and machine-learning-assisted reduced-order models.

Current research directions include upper-airway airflow analysis for obstructive sleep apnea, biofluid mechanics, pump and propeller performance, cavitation and erosion, natural fiber-reinforced composites, and computational workflows that support faster diagnosis and engineering decision-making.

Research Interests

  • Computational Fluid Dynamics and experimental validation
  • Biofluid engineering, pseudoaneurysm treatment analysis, and ureteral stent flow
  • Upper-airway CFD, obstructive sleep apnea, reduced-order modeling, and medical twin
  • Turbomachinery, booster pumps, submersible drainage pumps, rim-driven propellers, cavitation, and erosion
  • Composite materials, natural fiber-reinforced polymers, and additive manufacturing
  • Machine learning, Dynamic Mode Decomposition, Koopman theory, and reduced-order modeling
